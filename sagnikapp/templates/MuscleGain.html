Moves to Make the Most of Your

At-Home Workout



Muscle Gain

When the intermediate routine becomes a breeze, take a stab at these advanced moves.



Steps to Follow:



Press Up

Complete the same number of reps on each side.This move uses multiple muscle groups for maximum growth and strengthens your shoulder joints.

Directions:
1.Get down into a press-up position with your hands placed shoulder-width apart and back flat, so a straight line forms from your head to heels, via your glutes.
2. Lower your body until your chest is an inch from the ground then explosively drive up by fully extending your arms. That's on rep.





Skipping

The ultimate no-nonsense workout, jumping rope could be the most efficient form of cardio. A study that found just 10 minutes a day with the rope was similar to 30 minutes of jogging

Directions:
1.Grab the rope at both ends. Use your wrists to flick it round your body, jumping to clear the rope as it hits the ground.
2. Make the move more intense with double unders – letting the rope pass round your twice for every jump.





Dumbell Squat

Squats are an excellent all-round exercise and one of the best moves for building overall strength. Dumbbells let you concentrate on technique and work on your range of movement at low weight.

Directions:
1.Holding a dumbbell in each hand, position your legs shoulder width apart.
2. Keeping your head up and back straight, sit back into the squat until the dumbbells are an inch from the floor.
3. Focus on keeping your knees over your toes and chest out – don't arch your back or lean forward as you drop down.
Exhale, straighten your legs and return to the starting position.





Burpees

When it comes to burning fat at home, few moves can do better then the burpee. Perfect for frying fat with zero equipment, work these into your home workout routine to ramp up your heart rate or set yourself daily challenges.

Directions:
1.From a standing position squat down until your thighs are parallel to the floor and place your palms on the floor.
2. From there kick your feet back as far as you can while keeping your arms extended.
As soon as your feet land jump them back in towards your hands, then jump up into the air.
3. Land and immediately squat down to go into the next rep.





Jumping Lunges

Just like burpees, these are perfect for building up your cardiovascular system, but will also help you build faster, more powerful quadriceps. Ideal if your leg day has taken a hit.

Directions:
1. Lunge forward until your rear knee is almost touching the ground. Jump into the air, bringing your rear foot forward and the front foot back.
2. Land in a lunge and repeat.
3. Land on both feet simultaneously to cushion the impact on your joints.





Spiderman Press-Up

Perfect if you're looking to get some mobility in before a workout or just want to switch up your chest routine, Spiderman press-ups are the perfect alternative if you're looking to scale things up.

Directions:
1. Get into a traditional press-up position. Lower yourself toward the floor and bring your right knee to your right elbow, keeping it off the ground.
2. Press back up and return your leg to the starting position. Repeat with the alternate leg.





Plank

Planks are perfect for working your core in a way that keeps you injury-free and builds the flat six-pack you're after.

Directions:
1.Get in a press-up position, but rest on your forearms rather than your hands.
2. Make sure your back is straight and tense your abs and glutes. Hold without allowing your hips to sag.





Butterfly Crunches: 90 seconds

This Crunch Variation Will Light Up Your Core and Stretch Your Inner Thighs

Directions:
1. Lay on your back and crunch up so that your head is off the floor.
2. Remain crunched for the entire exercise.
3. Spread your arms out to the sides and in one fluid movement, bring your knees towards your chest and tap your fingers above your knees. – Repeat





Bench dips

This is easy to do on a chair, stair or coffee table. It works the arms, chest and shoulders and is great if you want people to notice that you've started working out as it

Directions:
1. Stand facing away from a bench, grab it with both hands at shoulder-width.
2.Extend your legs out in front of you. Slowly lower your body by flexing at the elbows until your arm at forearm create a 90 degree angle.
3.Using your triceps lift yourself back to the starting position.





Diamond Press-Ups

If standard press-ups are feeling too easy, try this. It'll lgoke your triceps and chest and challenge your form.
A tip: Keep your core locked to avoid sagging at the hips and putting stress on the lower back.

Directions:
1.Get in a press-up position and place your hands together so your index fingers and thumbs form a diamond.
2.Keep your back straight as you lower until your chest almost touches the floor then push back up to the start position.
